The IKEA Video Manuals dataset is a large-scale multimodal dataset with high-quality, spatial-temporal alignments of step-by-step instructions, 3D object representations, and real-world video demonstrations from the Internet. It provides 34,441 annotated video frames, aligning 36 IKEA manuals with 98 assembly videos for 6 furniture categories.

Dataset Overview

Key Features

  • Multiple instruction forms for assembly, including high-level assembly tree, instruction manuals, and assembly videos
  • Temporal alignment of instruction and assembly process at different granularities
  • Spatial alignment of instruction and assembly process in 3D
  • Diversity in assembly scenarios, encompassing various furniture types, designs, and processes
  • Complexity in real-world videos, capturing challenges such as camera changes, occlusions, and diverse backgrounds

Furniture Models

The IKEA Video Manuals dataset includes 36 furniture models from 6 categories:

  • Chairs (20 types)
  • Tables (8 types)
  • Benches (3 types)
  • Desks (1 type)
  • Shelves (1 type)
  • 3 miscellaneous items

Annotation

The IKEA Video Manuals dataset provides rich annotations at multiple levels:

  • Furniture-level annotations, including 3D models, part segmentation masks, and part poses
  • Video-level annotations, including camera parameters and video segmentation
  • Assembly step-level annotations, including hierarchical assembly trees
  • Frame-level annotations, including 2D part segmentation masks and 3D part poses
  • Manual-level annotations, including manual segmentation and part-conditioned pose estimation

Data Collection and Annotation

The IKEA Video Manuals dataset is built on top of the IKEA-Manual dataset and the IAW dataset. It collects 36 segmented 3D furniture models from the IKEA-Manual dataset and 98 associated assembly videos from the IAW dataset.

The dataset provides extensive annotations, including:

  • Fine-grained temporal segmentation of videos based on the construction of each sub-assembly
  • 2D image segmentation masks for 3D parts in sampled frames
  • 3D poses of furniture parts in video frames
  • Camera parameter estimation for each video segment
  • Hierarchical assembly trees representing the assembly process

Applications

The IKEA Video Manuals dataset demonstrates its utility on four fundamental furniture analysis tasks:

  1. Assembly plan generation
  2. Part-conditioned segmentation
  3. Part-conditioned pose estimation
  4. Shape assembly with instruction videos

For each application, the paper provides evaluation metrics and baseline methods, highlighting the challenges and opportunities for future research.
